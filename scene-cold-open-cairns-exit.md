# Cold Open — Cairns' Last Day (Beat Sheet)

INT. OFFICE SPACE — DAY. One year before the story. The floor holds maybe 20 people (more than we'll ever see again — the audience won't clock that until later).

**1. The box.** Cairns packs his desk into a printer-paper box. He's doing it loudly — narrating, performing. Nobody asked. A few heads pop over monitors like prairie dogs. Will and John among them.

**2. The speech nobody scheduled.** Cairns climbs half onto his chair and gives an exit speech to the floor. It's funny, a little cruel, mostly true: "None of you know what you do here. I checked. I asked Doug and he described a feeling." Scattered laughter. HR is not present. HR is an email.

**3. The robot boss rolls up.** Wheels heard before it's seen. The likeness delivers a warm, fluent farewell — "we're so grateful for your four years, Cairns" — perfect eye contact, little head tilt. Cairns stares at it, then past it, at the webcam light: "Tell him he could've called." Beat — 0.4 seconds. "I'll flag that upstream." Laugh from the floor; hold one extra second so it curdles.

**4. No plan (the anti-plant).** At the elevator, Will and John help carry nothing — they just came to stand. John: "So what's the plan down there?" Cairns, buzzing, invincible: "There is no plan. That's the plan. I'll figure it out when I land — that's the POINT, bro." No fixer, no contact, no name. His recklessness is the thing they admire and fear. (Michelle is deliberately NOT planted here — her name must only ever come out of the machine's mouth. When FaceTime-Cairns has a concierge who "handles everything" in Scene #1, John can clock it: "since when does Cairns have a plan?" Played as growth; actually the seam.)

**5. The last frame of him.** Doors close on Cairns mid-sentence — still talking, still selling. We never see the real Cairns in person again. (On rewatch, this is the last verified human Cairns in the film.)

**6. The desk.** The floor, quieter. Someone's already turned back to their screen. Hold on Cairns' empty desk — box gone, chair still spun. Then: TITLE CARD — "NOMAD" — and we cut to ONE YEAR LATER, the same floor, 9 people, motion-sensor lights clicking on ahead of Will. The desk is exactly as it was. Nobody ever sat there again.

## Function checklist

Comedy on first viewing, conveyor belt on second. Plants: the robot boss's "flag it upstream," the desk-shrine, the 0.4-second beat, Cairns' selling voice, and above all his NO-PLAN exit — so when FaceTime-Cairns produces Michelle, a fixer who "handles everything," the contradiction is sitting in plain sight. Michelle is never planted; her name only ever comes out of the machine's mouth. The one thing this scene must hide in plain sight: Cairns at Beat 4 of the erosion. His speech is the sound of a man who spent six months refreshing an empty queue, played as swagger.
