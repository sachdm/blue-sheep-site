# Log 03 — Development: The Office

First development session on NOMAD. Decisions made:

**Will's job.** Fraud ops — identity verification, half-automated. He clicks APPROVE on the machine's calls, 40 seconds a face. Give him one moment of real skill early: he catches a deepfake the system missed, off a human tell. Nobody cares. The audience remembers. His own likeness has to beat that tell in Act 3.

**The inciting crack — the erosion.** Nobody fires Will. Four beats spread across Act 1: the queue rebalance that feels like a gift; the accuracy report where "top human performer" enters his vocabulary; the edge-case queue rigged to make him look worse; then "Supervisor" — a man refreshing an empty queue like a phone. That's the night Cairns calls. He doesn't quit dramatically at the end of it — he texts Michelle something small and deniable. Nobody storms out of an erosion; they drift out.

**Cairns left first.** The erosion is a cycle and it got Cairns a year earlier — same four beats, off-screen. He's the canary, not just the lure. The scheme harvests people at exactly Beat 4, because that's when they're ripest. "You gotta get out here bro" isn't bragging; it's Cairns (or his likeness) talking to a man standing exactly where he stood.

**Cold open — Cairns' last day.** Six beats: the box; the exit speech nobody scheduled ("I asked Doug and he described a feeling"); the robot boss's warm farewell — "tell him he could've called" / "I'll flag that upstream"; the elevator; doors closing on Cairns mid-sell — the last verified human Cairns in the film; hold on the empty desk, title card, ONE YEAR LATER.

**The Michelle anti-plant.** Killed the idea of planting her name in the cold open — too convenient, muddies the timeline. Inverted instead: the real Cairns leaves with NO plan ("there is no plan, that's the plan, bro"). Michelle's name only ever comes out of the machine's mouth. When FaceTime-Cairns has a concierge who "handles everything," John clocks it — "since when does Cairns have a plan?" Played as growth. Actually the seam.

**The office itself.** Maintained emptiness, not ruins. Built for 400, holding 9, cleaned nightly, coffee auto-brewing for a full floor at 8:55. The robot boss's likeness is nicer than the real boss, so nobody resents it. John's mini-putt course lives deep in the dark zone — the happiest scene in the movie should ache a little.
