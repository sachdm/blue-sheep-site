# Thirteen Passes on the Master Script

Iterations over the rendered script through three lenses — the structural/temporal rigor associated with Nolan's school, the atmospheric image-first dread associated with Villeneuve's, and the procedural forensic coldness associated with Fincher's. Each pass: what the lens caught, and the verdict. Adopted changes landed in MASTER DRAFT v0.8; the script was re-rendered after.

---

**Pass 1 — structure: what does the audience hold in their hands?** The film is strictly linear; the lens asks if time can work harder. Full nonlinearity rejected — the erosion only works linearly, drip by drip. But the CLIMAX is two rituals in two countries: a man handed over in a clean room, a man decorating a dead office for a party. **ADOPTED: scenes 8 and 9 are now INTERCUT — the double ritual.** Door closes / banner goes up. Phone on a tray archiving itself / phone buzzing with the fake. The cut becomes the film's final argument: both men are attending a funeral only one of them can see.

**Pass 2 — image first: what does the film look like before it means anything?** The film currently opens on information (an office). The lens wants an image that teaches the eye the film's rule — images lie — before a word is spoken. **ADOPTED: a PROLOGUE IMAGE: full-frame, we open INSIDE paradise — a pool at golden hour, too perfect, water like poured glass, a laugh on the soundtrack landing 0.4 seconds late — one single frame stutters. TITLE: NOMAD. Then the office.** Twenty seconds. On first watch: beautiful. On rewatch: we opened inside a render.

**Pass 3 — rules: is the technology airtight?** The lens tolerates no hand-waving: why keep originals? where does the fidelity come from? The draft says "kept" but never shows the rule. **ADOPTED: one image, no dialogue — in the clean room, behind Will, a wall of small numbered lockers, dozens, most tagged. One tag we can read: a first name and a version number.** The rule (originals are retained as reference) becomes furniture. Nobody explains it. Airtight and silent.

**Pass 4 — dread is duration.** The tell (scene 6) resolves too fast on the page. The lens holds the frame past comfort. **ADOPTED: direction locked — after the wrong-eyed answer, the call does NOT end awkwardly. It continues, pleasantly, for a full further beat. Will has to perform normalcy INSIDE the frame with the thing wearing his friend, say a warm goodnight, and be told love you bro back — 0.4 late.** The horror is that nothing happens.

**Pass 5 — the environment is a character.** Mexico City is currently a backdrop; the building is an address. **ADOPTED: the building gets an APPROACH — seen three times, escalating: background of the subtitled conversation (unnoticed), across the street the day he checks it (ordinary), and finally from below on appointment day, monolithic, filling the frame.** Same building, three sizes. The trap grows in the lens as he nears it.

**Pass 6 — time needs a clock.** The office runs on clocks (8:55 pot, motion timers); paradise is deliberately timeless — that's the seduction. The lens sharpens it: **ADOPTED: Mexico contains NO visible clocks or times until Michelle's appointment message — the first specific time Will is given in the country.** Paradise abolishes time; the trap is the only thing that keeps it. The appointment notification should land like an alarm going off in Eden.

**Pass 7 — the emotional anchor needs an object.** The lens distrusts sentiment carried by dialogue; it wants mass. The bracket is good; it needs a physical partner. **ADOPTED: WILL'S PUTTER — John tapes Will's name on it during the packing scene and keeps it. Championship day, it leans against hole 7, reserved, unused. It's in the final frame with John.** The film's grief gets an object the camera can simply see.

**Pass 8 — reveal by sound, then image.** The two-likenesses discovery is the film's deepest cut and currently arrives visually. The lens stages it in audio first. **ADOPTED: in the render farm, Will hears HIS OWN LAUGH from the next row — his real laugh, the one we've heard at mini-putt — before he sees the screen.** He follows his own voice to the worst image in the film. Sound design owns the beat; the visual confirms it.

**Pass 9 — the opening bookend.** With the prologue image adopted, the lens asks for its rhyme. **ADOPTED: the final image adjusts — after HOLD ON JOHN, the cut to black is preceded by a single frame stutter, identical to the prologue's.** Blink and it's gone. The film ends the way it began: inside something rendered, or close enough that we can no longer tell. (Also answers: whose paradise was the prologue? By the end — possibly Will's.)

**Pass 10 — synthesis and subtraction.** Final pass, both lenses agree on the same discipline: after nine additions, cut anything that now double-states. Reviewed: the golf-sim boss cutaway (kept — it's the comedy the dread needs), the WFH mutiny (kept — load-bearing), the second "0.4" mention in scene 7 (TRIMMED — the standups line carries it), the operator-notices-reflection beat (kept, now rhymes with pass 3's lockers: witnessed, catalogued, irrelevant). Verdict: the film is one image leaner and two images heavier, and every addition is silent. Nothing added speaks.

**Pass 11 — procedure is photographed, not described.** The forensic lens: every capture in this film is a transaction, and transactions deserve INSERTS — crisp, macro, clinical. **ADOPTED: an insert-shot language for the whole film — the scanner's green line crossing a passport; a checkbox filling: ☑ REFERENCE RETENTION (the only time the rule is ever "stated," and it's furniture); initials on glass; the tray. Every deposit gets one cold close-up.** The film's violence is paperwork; shoot the paperwork like weapons.

**Pass 12 — synthetic at the edges.** The lens puts wrongness in the background grain, never the foreground. **ADOPTED: the "Trusting Your Tools" coaching module has a cheerful presenter who blinks at perfectly regular intervals.** Never mentioned, never lit, never framed as a reveal. Some of the audience sees it. That's the correct number.

**Pass 13 — the basement sequence.** Between the tell and the building, Will currently just... goes. The forensic lens demands the obsession be shown as METHOD. **ADOPTED: THE SCRUB — one tight nocturnal sequence: desk lamp, notes, side-by-sides. Old real videos of Cairns against the new calls, frame-stepped. Timestamps compared across time zones. The laugh isolated, waveformed, measured: 0.4. He does to his best friend exactly what he did at his desk in scene 2 — and gets the same verdict.** It's the fraud-ops skill turned all the way inward, and it earns the trip to the building.
