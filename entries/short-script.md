Blocking format: action and motivation, improv-ready, no written dialogue — with one exception: the pool call with Cairns plays as written (log 02, preserved word for word, marked below). Camera grammar from the award cut is noted per scene. Target 15–20 min.

## 1. COLD OPEN — INT. OFFICE SPACE — DAY (ONE YEAR AGO) — 90 sec

**Action:** Cairns packs his desk into a printer-paper box, loud, performing. Ten seconds of an exit speech nobody scheduled — its intent: leave as the sane one, the bigger the laugh the safer he feels. The ROBOT BOSS (monitor on wheels, warm AI likeness of the boss) glides in, delivers a fluent farewell. Cairns answers past the screen, to the webcam light. The machine takes 0.4 seconds — the first time we hear the delay — and promises to flag it upstream. The floor laughs; hold until it curdles.

At the elevator, Will and John flank him, carrying nothing. John's practical question: the plan. Cairns, in whatever words: there is no plan, that's the point. Doors close on him mid-sell. *(Last verified human Cairns in the film. No fixer. No name. Michelle must never exist in his mouth.)*

His chair, still spinning. **TITLE: NOMAD.** SUPER: ONE YEAR LATER.

**Sound:** establish the office's maintained dead air. Wheels on carpet heard before seen.

## 2. THE OFFICE — INT. OFFICE SPACE — MORNING — 2½ min

**Action:** Motion-sensor lights click on in sequence ahead of Will — a runway for nobody. He passes dark departments; passes Cairns' desk, untouched. The 8:55 coffee machine brews a full pot for a floor of nine; Will pours its only cup.

His job in one image: a queue of faces, machine verdicts, one button — APPROVE. Click. Click. A streak counter climbs.

**The skill beat and the demotion, fused:** a face comes through, machine-verified 98%. Will stops. Replays three seconds — the candidate asked about childhood, the eyes going nowhere, not doing the thing eyes do when a person actually remembers. He overrides: synthetic. The system checks. Concedes. His streak resets to zero — overrides break streaks. He looks up to tell someone; eight heads down. On his monitor, this month's metrics render: SYSTEM 99.1 / WILL 96.8, and a warm banner — top *human* performer.

**Will's motivation:** the four seconds of the catch are the only time today he is fully awake. The zeroed streak is the system fining him for being right. He archives all of it and clicks on.

**Camera:** the audience must learn the tell here, at desktop scale, so Call #2 can pay it at cinema scale.

## 4. THE DIAL + THE MOVE — NIGHT — 90 sec

**Action:** Will's apartment — a hotel he's lived in for six years. Laptop open on the river dashboard, running fine without him. He types, deletes, types: something small and deniable to Michelle — Cairns gave him her number, that's all. Sends. Face-down. Face-up. Three dots, instantly. Like she was already typing.

Then the permission-screen montage at speed: passport scan, selfie video "for the building," voice enrollment "for the intercom," a gait-capture walkthrough dressed as a tour-preference quiz. Each screen a cheerful ALLOW.

**Design intent:** this montage is the heist. Every biometric the ending needs is deposited here, on camera, with Will smiling. Nearly subliminal on first watch.

**Will's motivation:** he is not deciding to go; he is deciding to ask, which he can tell himself is not deciding. The text is the drift.

## Blocking totals

Nine scenes, ~16–18 minutes. Locations: one office floor (cheap, found — it should look maintained and dead), one Ohio apartment (one wall), one CDMX apartment + rooftop pool (rented for a day — which is literally the plot), the staging room (same apartment redressed, pool drained), one clean room. Two of the three calls share a set. The production's constraints and the scheme's constraints are the same joke — let them be.

## Performance keys

Cairns: two calibrated performances a half-degree apart — the terrified man under the volume (scene 1) and the model's warm rendition (scenes 3, 6). Cast for the eyes, not the comedy. Will: the four-second awakenings (scenes 2 and 6) must rhyme exactly. John: no irony anywhere in him; the last shot only works if his delight is total.
