# NOMAD — Act Two (Working Script, Draft 0.1)

Same format as Act One: no written dialogue — action plus the motivation under each beat, built for improv. Act Two spans from Michelle's first reply to the trap closing. Structure: 2A the frictionless move → 2B the honeymoon → MIDPOINT the tell → 2C the unraveling → the capture.

**Language rule for the whole act:** Spanish spoken between locals is subtitled. Spanish spoken *to Will* is not. The audience always knows more than he does. The film is bilingual; Will isn't.

---

## 2A — THE FRICTIONLESS MOVE

## MICHELLE (rules of the character)

Will never meets Michelle in person in this act. She is a warm voice, a profile photo, instant replies at any hour — 2 AM his time, 4 AM hers, no timezone friction, which no one clocks because being always-on is normal now. Her competence is the seduction: every problem dissolves before Will finishes describing it.

**Michelle's motivation (play her sincere):** whoever or whatever she is, she behaves like the best concierge on earth. The performance is not sinister. That's what makes it sinister.

## INT. WILL'S APARTMENT / PHONE SCREENS — NIGHT (CONTINUOUS FROM ACT ONE)

**Action:** The three dots resolve. Over days compressed into a sequence: Michelle handles everything. Lease broken, flights found, a "residency fast-track" appointment booked. Each ask requires one small deposit of Will: a passport scan. A selfie video "for the building." A voice enrollment "for the intercom." A recorded walkthrough of his gait and mannerisms disguised as a "video tour preference quiz." Played entirely as convenience montage — the modern texture of giving yourself away one permission screen at a time.

**Design intent:** every biometric the Act Three likeness needs is collected here, on camera, with Will smiling. On rewatch this sequence is the heist.

## INT. OFFICE SPACE — WILL'S LAST DAY

**Action:** No speech, no box performance — the anti-Cairns exit. Will doesn't quit; he goes "remote." The firm doesn't care where the Supervisor sits. The Robot Boss delivers a farewell anyway — and it is, word for word, rhythm for rhythm, the farewell it gave Cairns in the cold open. Same head tilt. Same 0.4 seconds. Will hears it and almost catches it.

**Will's motivation:** to leave without it counting as leaving. Remote status is his deniability — he can tell himself nothing has been decided.

**John's motivation:** he helps Will pack the apartment. No wound, no warning, one genuine request buried in the logistics: come back for the mini-putt championship. He means it, and it will matter in Act Three.

## INT. DEAD SALES DEPARTMENT — NIGHT — THE GOODBYE

**Action:** One last round of the course, lights off in the rest of the building, phone flashlights. Will wins. John concedes hole 7 was rigged for him to win it. They don't hug so much as stand near each other holding putters.

**Tone:** the second-happiest scene in the movie, and the last honest one for a long time.

## 2B — THE HONEYMOON

## EXT./INT. MEXICO CITY — ARRIVAL SEQUENCE

**Action:** The ad comes true. The apartment is better than the photos. The fridge is stocked — with his exact brands, the coffee he buys, the hot sauce he once mentioned to Cairns years ago. He experiences this as delight. It is data leakage wearing a bow.

**Design intent:** the trap's tell is over-fit. Everything is slightly too tailored, the way an algorithm's gift is. Nothing generic ever happens to Will here.

## MONTAGE — THE LIFE

**Action:** Rooftop workday: the river dashboard scrolls on his laptop next to a pool. He refreshes the empty action queue out of habit, then — for the first time — closes the laptop mid-refresh. Tacos, mezcal, the nomad social scene: a floating cast of Americans, every one of them mid-erosion. A guy who has been "about to launch" for three years. A woman with a podcast about leaving. Nobody can say what anybody does. (Echo of Cairns' exit speech — nobody asked Doug here either.)

**The alternation begins:** cut against Will's montage, the same locations from the other side. The barista opening the shop she can no longer afford to live above. Subtitled conversations Will walks through like weather. Graffiti we can read and he can't. Not lectures — texture. The city is having a conversation about Will that Will cannot hear.

## THE POOL PARTY — THE FALSE PEAK

**Action:** A rooftop party that recreates the frame of Cairns' Scene #1 call — the pool, the drinks, the abundance, someone thrown in the water. Will, mid-laugh, catches his own reflection in a phone screen held up to film him and realizes, with pleasure, that he is inside the ad. He toasts it.

**Will's motivation:** vindication. The erosion is a distant country. He was right to come. This is the top of the arc; everything after descends.

## MIDPOINT — THE TELL

## INT. WILL'S APARTMENT — NIGHT — THE CALL WITH CAIRNS

**Action:** Will keeps trying to see Cairns in person; Cairns is always about to travel, always "next week." Fine — a call instead. Same poolside frame as ever. And somewhere in it, Will asks a nothing question — a real memory, private texture, something like Doug's speech or a night only the two of them know — and the answer comes back *correct but transcribed*: the facts right, the eyes wrong. The eyes go nowhere. They don't do the thing eyes do when a person actually remembers.

Will's face changes the way it changed at his desk in Act One. Four seconds of being fully awake. Then he laughs it off, says goodnight, closes the laptop, and sits very still.

**Why this is the midpoint:** his one skill — the thing the machine fined him for — just flagged his best friend as synthetic. He cannot un-see it, and he cannot say it out loud, because saying it means everything here is the ad.

**The likeness's motivation:** it isn't failing. It's performing Cairns at 99.1%. Will is the edge case.

## 2C — THE UNRAVELING

## SEQUENCE — WILL RUNS LIVENESS CHECKS ON HIS BEST FRIEND

**Action:** Will does what fraud ops does — quietly, ashamed of himself. Spontaneity tests on calls: asks Cairns to grab a specific object, step into daylight, do the thing with the— he improvises human verification protocols disguised as banter. The likeness handles most of it. Where it can't, the call conveniently degrades — girls arrive, connection drops, "gotta run bro." The system's evasions are indistinguishable from Cairns' personality. That's the horror: flakiness was already his character. The model chose its skin well.

**Will's motivation:** to be wrong. Every test is designed hoping it fails to confirm. This is the first time in the film he wants a face to be real rather than wanting his streak.

**Michelle's counter-motion:** her check-ins tighten by a degree. Same warmth, higher frequency. Somewhere in here she gently mentions things Will did that he didn't tell her about. Concierge or surveillance — the film should refuse to let us tell the difference yet.

## THE LOCAL THREAD CONVERGES

**Action:** The barista — or the neighbor, whoever we've been cutting to all act — turns out to live near the address Cairns "lives" at. Through the subtitled side of the film, we (not Will) have been hearing about that building for an hour: nobody lives there; men bring equipment at night; the pool on that roof was rented out for a shoot once, months ago, one single day.

Will finally goes. The building is real. The apartment is a staging set: the pool from every call, drained. Ring lights. A rack of shirts — Cairns' shirts, the loud ones, pressed and tagged. The girls from Scene #1, on a callback sheet pinned to a corkboard, dated eight months ago. One day of shooting, an infinite vacation.

**Design intent:** no violence yet, no chase. The scariest thing in the room is the drained pool.

## GLIMPSE — THE OPERATION

**Action:** Will, hiding as someone arrives, sees one floor down through a service window: rows of workstations. On each screen, a face he half-recognizes from the nomad scene — likenesses of dozens of foreigners, each one at work, attending standups, laughing 0.4 seconds late. A farm of borrowed lives. The workers tending them are calm, professional, young. Somewhere, someone is dubbing joy into a likeness's laugh, and it sounds like the office coffee machine: maintained.

**The group's texture (hold the full argument for Act Three):** not cartel, not chaos. Organized, quiet, ideological around the edges — a poster, a phrase of graffiti we've seen all act, now indoors. They are monetizing the colonizers. The film should let that thought land unanswered.

## ACT TWO CLOSE — THE HANDOVER

## INT. WILL'S APARTMENT — NIGHT

**Action:** Will, wrecked, starts to act like a witness: drafts an email to his own firm's fraud department — his old queue, his own language, SUSPECTED SYNTHETIC. Hovers over send.

His phone lights up first. Michelle. Warm as ever: his "final residency appointment" has been moved up. Tomorrow. The building he was just inside.

**Action, closing sequence:** Will considers running — airport tabs open, John's contact on screen (he doesn't call; protecting John or ashamed, actor's choice). In the end he goes to the appointment — because the fraud-ops mind wants confirmation, because running requires believing it fully, because the appointment is framed as the thing a person with nothing to fear would attend. A clean room with good light and a chair. Cameras he recognizes — the same models his own firm uses for liveness capture. Forms already filled in. They have everything; they've had everything since the permission screens of 2A. The capture is not an abduction. It's a *completion*. The model finished training, and the original is now redundant.

The door closes politely.

## BUTTON — INT. OFFICE SPACE, OHIO — DAY

**Action:** The river dashboard scrolls, fine. John's phone buzzes at the mini-putt course. A message from Will — cadence perfect, warmth slightly too on-message: mexico is crazy, you gotta come out here, bro.

John, delighted, starts typing back.

**CUT TO BLACK. END OF ACT TWO.**

## Open questions for Act Three

- John is now the target and the detection system: his dweeby loyalty is the one instrument the likeness can't calibrate against. How early does he sense it?
- Is Michelle a person, a role passed between operators, or a likeness herself? (The instant three dots at 2 AM cut both ways.)
- The real Cairns: alive in the building? Already discarded? Act Three must pay this, and the answer decides the film's temperature.
- Will's play from inside: he is the only captive who knows how detection works — he taught us the tell in Act One. The likeness of Will has one flaw: it can't want to be wrong.
- The email draft to the fraud department: unsent, or sent and auto-flagged as synthetic because it doesn't match Will's baseline? (Cruelest available irony — hold for Act Three.)
