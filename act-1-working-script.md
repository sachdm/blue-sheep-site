# NOMAD — Act One (Working Script, Draft 0.2)

Format: no written dialogue. Each scene is action plus the motivation driving it — objectives, obstacles, subtext — so every beat can be improvised. Where a character speaks, what's given is the *intent* of the speech, never the words. (The scripted version of Scene #1 lives in log 02 if ever needed.)

---

## COLD OPEN — INT. OFFICE SPACE — DAY (ONE YEAR AGO)

A floor built for 400, holding maybe 20. Alive enough that we don't yet know it's dying.

**Action:** Cairns packs his desk into a printer-paper box, loudly, performing it. Heads pop up over monitors. He ends up half-standing on his chair giving an exit speech nobody scheduled. The Robot Boss — a monitor on a wheeled stand running the warm AI likeness of their boss Greg — glides in and delivers a farewell. Cairns addresses his reply past the screen, to the webcam light itself. The machine takes a fraction of a beat too long, then responds with corporate warmth. The floor laughs. Hold until it curdles.

**Cairns' motivation:** to leave as the sane one. His speech is a roast of the office but the engine under it is six months of refreshing an empty queue — he needs witnesses to a decision he's terrified by, so he performs certainty. The bigger the laugh, the safer he feels.

**The floor's motivation:** to enjoy the show without catching what it implies about them.

**Robot Boss's behavior:** warm, fluent, 0.4 seconds late on every human rhythm. It cannot be insulted, which is the insult.

## INT. ELEVATOR BANK — CONTINUOUS

**Action:** Will and John flank Cairns at the elevator, helping carry nothing — they came to stand. John asks the practical question: what's the plan down there. Cairns' answer, in whatever words: there is no plan, the absence of a plan is the point. The doors close on him mid-sentence, still selling. *(On rewatch: the last verified human Cairns in the film.)*

**Cairns' motivation:** recklessness as identity. He needs them to admire the leap, because if they worry, the fear gets in.

**Will's motivation:** to be near the event without being implicated by it. He says almost nothing. He's taking notes he doesn't know he's taking.

**John's motivation:** simple, unjealous curiosity. He asks about the plan because he'd have one.

**RULE ESTABLISHED HERE: no fixer, no contact, no name. Michelle must never exist in the real Cairns' mouth.**

## INT. OFFICE SPACE — MOMENTS LATER

**Action:** The floor already back to work. Cairns' chair still slowly spinning. Hold on the empty desk.

**TITLE CARD: NOMAD**

## INT. OFFICE SPACE — MORNING (PRESENT DAY)

**SUPER: ONE YEAR LATER**

**Action:** Same floor. Nine people. Motion-sensor lights click on in sequence ahead of Will — a runway for nobody. He passes dark departments; passes Cairns' desk, untouched, an accidental shrine. The coffee machine auto-brews a full pot for the whole floor at 8:55; Will pours the only cup it will give anyone. Somewhere in the dark zone the Robot Boss glides between empty desks, greeting them by name.

**World rule:** this is maintained emptiness, not ruins. Everything still works. That's the horror — nothing here can be mourned because nothing has technically died.

**Will's state:** not sad. Rehearsed. A man who has optimized a routine inside something he refuses to examine.

## INT. OFFICE SPACE — WILL'S DESK — DAY

**Action:** Will's job in one image: a queue of faces, the machine's verdict under each, one button — APPROVE. Click, click, click. Forty seconds a face. A streak counter climbs in the corner of the dashboard.

**Will's motivation:** the streak. It's the only score in his life currently going up. He'd never admit he cares about it.

## INT. OFFICE SPACE — WILL'S DESK — LATER

**THE SKILL BEAT — protect this.**

**Action:** A recorded interview face comes through, machine-verified at 98%. Will stops. Replays three seconds. The candidate is asked about his childhood; the eyes go nowhere — they don't do the thing eyes do when a person actually remembers. Will overrides: flags it synthetic. The system checks, concedes: confirmed. And his streak counter resets to zero — overrides break streaks. He looks around to tell someone. Eight heads down. A Tuesday.

**Will's motivation in the moment:** not duty — recognition. He *sees* something the machine can't, and for four seconds he is fully awake. The zeroed streak is the system fining him for being right.

**Why it's here:** the audience must know exactly what Will can see, so that in Act 3 his own likeness has to beat the tell he taught us.

## THE EROSION — BEAT 1: THE REBALANCE

**Action:** An all-staff email about "queue optimization — no action needed," archived mid-sip. Will's queue is thinner. He finishes at 3:40 and is mildly pleased.

**Design intent:** the erosion's first move must feel like a gift. No menace. The audience should be as fooled as Will.

## INT. DEAD SALES DEPARTMENT — MINI-PUTT — DAY

**Action:** Deep in the dark zone: desks pushed to walls, holes cut in the carpet. John unveils hole 7 of his mini-putt course — it uses the old CEO's office door as a ramp. Will laughs for real, the only time today.

**John's motivation:** not denial, not coping — genuine contentment. He is making something human out of the abandonment, locally, harmlessly, with a friend. The film should be quietly embarrassed that he might be right.

**Will's motivation:** to borrow John's peace for an hour without ever asking how he got it.

**Tone note:** happiest scene in the movie; it should ache a little.

## THE EROSION — BEAT 2: THE ACCURACY REPORT

**Action:** Monthly metrics meeting — nine humans, three telepresence units, one guy on a phone propped against a mug. The Robot Boss warmly presents accuracy numbers: the system just above 99, Will just below 97. It singles Will out for praise with a phrase that quietly welds the word "human" into his job title. John congratulates him and means it, which is worse.

**Robot Boss's design:** the praise is sincere. There is no villain in the room. There is no room.

**Will's motivation:** to accept the compliment while privately doing the math on what it categorizes him as.

## THE EROSION — BEAT 3: THE EDGE CASES

**Action:** Will's queue drops to thirty faces a day — all edge cases, the 51% calls, the machine's leftovers. His live accuracy ticker sinks as he works. The system feeds him only its hardest calls and grades him on the curve it created.

Then: Will walking alongside the gliding Robot Boss, trying to explain a feedback loop to a monitor at walking pace. The machine hears his frustration and will flag it upstream. Cut to Greg's phone at home: the complaint arrives auto-summarized to two words and a dash, and a thumb swipes it away.

**Will's motivation:** he isn't asking for his job back — he's asking the system to admit the numbers lie. He wants the record corrected more than the outcome changed. That's what makes him harvestable.

## THE EROSION — BEAT 4: SUPERVISOR

**Action:** A confetti-graphic email: new title, "Verification Supervisor," and a raise the email itself calls an adjustment. His new dashboard is a river of the machine's decisions scrolling too fast to read, with one button — INTERVENE — and fine print that makes every intervention cost him a coaching module. Will watches the river. Refreshes an empty action queue. Refreshes it again, the way you refresh a phone. The 8:55 pot steams. Lights click off department by department on their timers.

**Design intent:** the promotion is the firing. No one did anything to him; there is no one to be angry at. This is the state the scheme harvests.

**THIS is the night Cairns calls.**

## SCENE #1 — THE CALL *(improv beats; scripted version in log 02)*

**Action:** Will at his desk, Cairns on the screen — poolside, food, drink, two girls. Cairns sells the life: everything cheap, everything doubled, work setup intact but now with margaritas. The girls escalate from decoration to distraction to physically pulling him from the frame. Somewhere in the flow, Cairns makes the offer: he's tight with someone named Michelle who handles everything — say the word. He's dragged off, shouting affection. The screen stays on: the three of them run to the pool, Cairns throws a girl in. Will watches. A tinge of jealousy sprouting in his expression. John arrives holding two beers, catches the tail end, registers mild appreciation — and one flicker of something off: Cairns, with a plan? Later, at the mini-putt course, John allows that it doesn't sound too bad — and admits, without shame, that he's never left the state.

**Cairns' (the likeness's) motivation:** this is a recruitment script wearing Cairns' personality. Its objective is one word from Will. It sells the way ads sell — abundance, scarcity of excuses, social proof in a bikini. Every improvised line should be something a model trained on Cairns *would* generate: his cadence, his bro-isms, slightly too on-message. It works because this exact script worked on Cairns.

**The seam (do not underline):** the real Cairns had no plan; this Cairns has a concierge. Michelle's name only ever comes out of the machine's mouth. John clocks the contradiction as trivia, not alarm.

**Will's motivation:** to consume the fantasy while performing detachment for John. The jealousy is the tell; closing the tab fast is the confession.

**John's motivation:** to share a beer with his friend. His "never left the state" is not a wound — it's a fact he's at peace with, which Will cannot comprehend tonight.

## END OF ACT ONE — THE DIAL

## INT. WILL'S APARTMENT — NIGHT

**Action:** First look at Will's home: a hotel he's lived in for six years — deliberate opposite of John's well-lived-in corner of the office. Will on the couch, laptop open, watching the dashboard's river run fine without him, the way you watch an aquarium. He picks up the phone. Types, deletes, types. Sends Michelle something small and deniable — Cairns gave him her number, that's all. Sets the phone face-down. Turns it face-up. Three dots appear instantly. Like she was already typing.

**Will's motivation:** he is not deciding to go. He is deciding to *ask*, which he can tell himself is not deciding. Nobody storms out of an erosion; they drift out. The text is the drift.

**The dots' job:** the trap's first visible muscle movement. She was already typing.

**CUT TO BLACK. END OF ACT ONE.**
